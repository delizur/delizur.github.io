Guitar Project

Handmade electric guitar

CNCWoodworking

I built a fully functioning electric guitar with the help of a wood CNC, wood routers, and hand tools. The body was cut from a solid piece of alder, with the rough shape cut by a CNC and finer shaping done with a router and hand files. The project gave me invaluable experience working with CNCs – setting up toolpaths and ensuring proper machine setup and operation. This project also developed my troubleshooting abilities, as well as resilience when dealing with frustrations in a project. There were numerous occasions when the CNC router I used would malfunction, effectively ruining the piece of wood I had carefully prepared. Through some digging on forums along with my own experimentation, I was able to find both mechanical and software issues in the machine, which caused it to behave irregularly. This project was an amazing opportunity for me to combine my engineering skills with my love for guitar. It also taught me that no matter what, there always exists a solution to a problem – it simply takes some perseverance and creativity to get there.
